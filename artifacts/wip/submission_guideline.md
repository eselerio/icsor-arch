## Scientific Study Contract

The final tables, figures, narrative values, and supplement must all derive from one benchmark contract:

- 10,000 accepted in-distribution ASM2d-TSN steady states;
- 22 inputs: hydraulic retention time, aeration, and 20 influent components;
- 20 effluent-component targets in the fixed manuscript order;
- eleven nested totals: 500, 1450, 2400, 3350, 4300, 5250, 6200, 7150, 8100, 9050, and 10,000;
- one seed-42 nested order and persistent five-fold assignment shared by every model;
- nine conventional regressors plus one TabICLv2 method;
- raw and projected predictions evaluated as paired outputs on identical rows;
- COD, TN, TP, and TSS calculated only after component prediction through the common composition map;
- separate mild and severe OOD strata generated by new mechanistic simulations; and
- no access to OOD responses during selection, preprocessing, context construction, or refitting.

The mechanistic simulator must evaluate all 28 process rates without an artificial rate ceiling or rate clipping. Bounds on state variables and initial guesses are distinct from rate handling. Confirm this condition before accepting the final simulated dataset.

## Metrics and Fold Summaries

The primary aggregate metrics operate on component residuals standardized by the applicable training-fold component standard deviation:

$$
e_{ij}=\frac{\hat c_{ij}-c_{out,ij}}{\sigma_j},
$$

$$
\mathrm{nMSE}=\frac{1}{20n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=1}^{20}e_{ij}^2,
\qquad
\mathrm{nRMSE}=\sqrt{\mathrm{nMSE}},
\qquad
\mathrm{nMAE}=\frac{1}{20n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=1}^{20}|e_{ij}|.
$$

Use the exact labels `nMSE`, `nRMSE`, and `nMAE` whenever these definitions are reported. Do not relabel them as aggregate RMSE, effective RMSE, test RMSE, MSE, or MAE. Physical-unit component or composite errors may use MSE, RMSE, and MAE only when the target and unit are explicit.

For every model--size cell:

1. retain all five outer-fold values;
2. calculate the arithmetic fold mean;
3. calculate the sample standard deviation with denominator four; and
4. report the descriptive summary as `five-fold mean ± sample SD`.

No p-values, stars, confidence limits, or hypothesis-test claims belong in this descriptive benchmark. Narrative comparisons should refer to lower or higher displayed values and should not imply a tested ordering.

## Physical Diagnostics

Compute physical diagnostics in the 20-component space before deriving COD, TN, TP, or TSS.

- Conservation residual: $v_{mc,i}=\lVert Ac_i-b_i\rVert_2$.
- Conservation violation: $v_{mc,i}>10^{-10}$.
- Non-negativity violation: any component below $-10^{-10}$.
- Standardized negative magnitude: $v_{nn,i}^{*}=\lVert\min(c_i\oslash\sigma,0)\rVert_1$.
- Projection effect: $\Delta\mathrm{nRMSE}=\mathrm{nRMSE}_{projected}-\mathrm{nRMSE}_{raw}$.
- Standardized displacement: $d_c^*=\lVert(\tilde c-c_{raw})\oslash\sigma\rVert_2$.

Retain raw and projected diagnostics on identical samples. Report mean, median, maximum, and frequency where planned; a low mean must not conceal rare large violations.

## Timing Contract

Partition timing into:

- preprocessing and conventional fitting;
- TabICLv2 context preparation;
- raw batch inference;
- projection alone;
- end-to-end projected inference; and
- hyperparameter search, reported separately.

Use the deterministic 512-row timing workload described in the manuscript. Apply five warm-up calls followed by 30 synchronized repetitions to each timed path. Use the median repetition latency within each fold, divide it by 512 for milliseconds per sample, and summarize the five fold medians as mean $\pm$ sample SD. TabICLv2 receives each complete timing partition in one call per component while retaining internal batch size eight and the fixed CUDA float32 configuration above.

Store each fold timing and report the descriptive five-fold mean ± sample SD. Record hardware, thread limits, package versions, and peak device memory with the final timing results.
